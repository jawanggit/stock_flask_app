Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA)

ARIMA is a forecasting model that utilizes time series based data to model the autocorrelations in the data. To improve the forecasting accuracy of an ARIMA model, the trend and seasonality in the time-series data is removed.

Data Prep and Model Tuning

The stock prices for DAL were rescaled using a log transformation in order to reduce the skewness in the distribution. Additionally, a first difference of the transformed stock prices was taken to achieve a stationary time series.

An autocorrelation and partial autocorrelation plot of the first differences was created to determine the model's parameters for Autoregressive (p), Integrated (d), and Moving Average (q).

Evaluation

The ARIMA model dramatically overpredicted the Daily Low each day which resulted in entering the market at far too high of price. The overall returns using ARIMA predictions resulted in a loss of over 300% during the 3 month trading window.
